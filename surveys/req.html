First, provide request-type questions to relay to a friend, John, via virtual assistant, such as:

Ask John, would he mind getting me some milk? (1)

Ask John if he could get going soon. (2)

Ask John to go for a walk before tomorrow's workout. (3)

Then, provide the virtual assistant's relayed message to John, from the perspective of the virtual assistant. Assuming your name is David, the above sentences would be converted as following:

Virtual Assistant converted output of (1): Hi David, John asks, would you mind getting me some milk?

Virtual Assistant converted output of (2): David, John would like to know if you could get going soon.

Virtual Assistant converted output of (3): Hi David, John asks you to go for a walk before tomorrow's workout.

Provide 4 dialogs of you sending a message with a request to a recipient via virtual assistant, and corresponding virtual assistant's converted messages to the recipient.

Requests are utterances that a sender requests an action from a recipient. There are a few queue phrases and words associated with requests. Below are some examples for each.

  • Ask (recipient) for (noun) - ask jason for more snacks
  • Ask (recipient) to (noun or action) - ask nate to do the dishes
  • Tell (recipient) to (noun or action) - tell simi to get the plane tickets already
  • Invite (recipient) to (noun or action) - invite nora to catch up with me at the reception
  • Invite (recipient) for (noun) - invite nora for coffee

Here are some examples of how these messages are converted via virtual assistant to relay to a messenger. Note that there can be some variations and here are some acceptable examples.

Example 1 - ask to

You/Sender (isabelle): can you ask alex to pick up some ice cream on the way home?

Virtual Assistant (variation 1): hi alex, isabelle wants you to pick up some ice cream on the way home.

Virtual Assistant (variation 2): hi alex, isabelle is asking you to pick up some ice cream on the way home.

Virtual Assistant (variation 3): hey alex, isabelle is requesting you to pick up some ice cream on the way home.

Virtual Assistant (variation 4): hey alex, isabelle would like you to pick up some ice cream on the way home.

Example 2 - ask for

You/Sender (rachel): please ask my husband for coffee.

Virtual Assistant (variation 1): hi jonah, rachel is asking you for coffee.

Virtual Assistant (variation 2): hi jonah, rachel wants to ask for coffee.

Virtual Assistant (variation 3): hi jonah, rachel would like coffee.

Virtual Assistant (variation 4): hi jonah, rachel is requesting coffee.

Example 3 - tell to

You/Sender (David): tell nick to get there a little early

Virtual Assistant (variation 1): hey nick, david is telling you to get there a little early

Virtual Assistant (variation 2): hi nick, david would like you to get there a little early

Virtual Assistant (variation 3): hey nick, david wants you to get there a little early

Virtual Assistant (variation 4): hey nick, david is requesting you to get there a little early

Example 4 - invite to

You/Sender (vera): please invite sam to go to the movies with me

Virtual Assistant (variation 1): hi sam, vera wants to invite you to go to the movies with them.

Virtual Assistant (variation 2): hey sam, vera invites you to go to the movies with them.

Virtual Assistant (variation 3): hey sam, vera would like to invite you to go to the movies with them.

Virtual Assistant (variation 4): hi sam, vera is inviting you to go to the movies with them.

Example 5 - invite for

You/Sender (ismail): please invite nora to my reception.

Virtual Assistant (variation 1): hi nora, ismail would like to invite you to their reception.

Virtual Assistant (variation 2): hey nora, ismail is inviting you to their reception.

Virtual Assistant (variation 3): hi nora, ismail wants you to come to their reception.

Virtual Assistant (variation 4): hi nora, ismail wants to invite you to their reception.
