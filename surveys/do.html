Questions involving "do" verbs

do-questions involve questions with variations on do-verbs with following gramatical structures.
  • do question: [do/does/did] + [subject] + [verb] + [object]

    • ex) ask john, does jessica have a coffee machine?

  • do question with negation: [don't/doesn't/didn't/do not/does not/did not] + [subject] + [verb] + [object]

    • ex) ask john didn't she have an appointment yesterday?

  • wh-do question: [what/where/who/whose/why/which/how] + [do/does/did] + [subject] + [verb] + [object]

    • ex) ask john which did you like better?

  • wh-do question with negation: [what/where/who/whose/why/which/how] + [don't/doesn't/didn't/do not/does not/did not] + [verb] + [object]

    • ex) ask john who didn't you like?

  • wh-object-do question: [what/where/who/whose/why/which/how] + [object] + [do/does/did] + [subject] + [verb]

    • ex) ask john which team did she pick?

  • wh-object-do question with negation: [what/where/who/whose/why/which/how] + [object] + [don't/doesn't/didn't/do not/does not/did not] + [verb] + [object]

    • ex) ask john what kind of food doesn't she like?
